Privacy Policy (UK GDPR)

Last Updated: 12/02/2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Vardags (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, processes, and protects personal data processed by our internal QuickBooks Online integration (“Integration”). This processing is performed in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Data Controller

Vardags is the Data Controller for all personal data processed through the Integration.

2. Data We Process

2.1 Accounting & Financial Data

  • Customer and vendor contact details
  • Invoice, payment, and transaction records

2.2 Internal User Data

  • Names, emails, employee identifiers
  • System login and authentication activity

2.3 Technical Data

  • API logs and diagnostic information
  • Timestamps and system metadata

3. Purpose of Processing

We process data to operate internal business systems, maintain financial accuracy, comply with legal obligations, support audits, and ensure operational continuity.

4. Lawful Basis (UK GDPR Article 6)

  • Legitimate Interests: internal financial and operational processes
  • Legal Obligation: accounting and auditing requirements
  • Contractual Necessity: fulfilling employment or supplier obligations

5. Data Retention

We retain financial and operational data according to legal retention periods and internal policies. Logs are kept only as long as necessary.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell or disclose data externally except to:

  • Intuit (QuickBooks Online) — for API operations
  • Microsoft Azure — for secure infrastructure hosting
  • Regulators or auditors — when required by law

7. International Transfers

Where data is transferred outside the UK, we use approved UK GDPR safeguards such as:

  • UK Addendum to Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Compliance mechanisms authorised by the ICO

8. Security Measures

We apply encryption, access controls, secure token storage, audit logging, and least‑privilege permissions across the Integration.

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you may request:

  • Access to your personal data
  • Correction of inaccurate data
  • Erasure where applicable
  • Restriction or objection to processing
  • Data portability (where relevant)

Requests can be made by contacting Vardags.
